# Portfolio Website Style Recommendation for AuroraMOSSdesigns

A strong fit would be a **biophilic “woodland atelier” portfolio site**: calm, tactile, natural, slightly magical, and more gallery-like than a typical ecommerce store.

The Etsy shop is built around **preserved moss wall art, framed moss pieces, tree bark, mushrooms, centerpieces, pinecones, driftwood, birch logs, cottagecore, woodland, dark academia, and natural textures**. The artist also describes working with “forest, ocean, and other natural textures,” and the shop has categories like **One-Of-A-Kind**, **Framed Moss Art**, and **Pots**.

## Best overall style: Natural gallery + handmade studio

Think:

**forest floor + art gallery + artisan studio**

The site should not feel like a generic Shopify store. It should feel like entering a quiet studio where each piece is handmade, textural, and one-of-a-kind.

## Visual direction

Use a **soft earthy palette**:

- Moss green
- Deep forest green
- Warm bark brown
- Birch/off-white
- Stone grey
- Small accents of mushroom beige, rust, or dark academia burgundy

A good palette could be:

```text
Forest green: #1F3A2D
Moss green:   #6F8F3D
Bark brown:   #6B4A35
Birch cream:  #F4EFE6
Stone grey:   #8D8A80
Charcoal:     #1E1E1B
```

## Layout style

I would use a **minimal editorial gallery layout**, with large product photography and lots of breathing room.

The homepage could be:

1. **Full-screen hero image**  
   A close-up of moss, bark, mushrooms, or a finished wall piece.  
   Headline example:  
   **“Preserved moss art inspired by the forest floor.”**

2. **Short artist statement**  
   Something warm and human, not too commercial.  
   Example:  
   *Handmade in British Columbia using preserved moss, wood, bark, stone-like textures, and natural forms.*

3. **Featured collections**  
   - One-of-a-kind pieces  
   - Framed moss art  
   - Centerpieces  
   - Custom work

4. **Portfolio/gallery section**  
   Large masonry-style grid, because the pieces are physical, textural, and varied.

5. **Process section**  
   Show close-ups: moss selection, bark, pinecones, arrangement, framing, packaging.

6. **Custom orders / commissions**  
   Etsy already offers custom orders, so the website should make that feel more premium.

7. **Trust signals**  
   Mention Etsy rating, reviews, sales, and Vancouver/BC location, because the shop already has strong credibility.

## Typography

I would avoid anything too sleek or techy. Better choices:

**Option A — elegant natural**

- Headings: Cormorant Garamond, Playfair Display, or Libre Baskerville
- Body: Inter, Source Sans 3, or Lora

**Option B — modern artisan**

- Headings: Fraunces or Cormorant
- Body: DM Sans or Karla

**Option C — dark academia / woodland**

- Headings: Cormorant Garamond
- Body: Spectral or Lora

The typography should feel handmade and artistic, but still readable.

## Three style options that would fit

### 1. Light woodland gallery

Best for broader appeal and sales.

Cream background, soft greens, warm wood tones, clean product cards, large images. This would make the art feel fresh, calming, and giftable.

Good for: home decor buyers, plant lovers, wedding gifts, housewarming gifts.

### 2. Dark forest / dark academia

This could work very well because some products already use “dark academia,” “gothic mushroom,” and “dark cottagecore” language.

Dark green or charcoal background, dramatic photography, warm highlights, close-up textures. More moody and premium.

Good for: gothic decor, mushroom art, dark cottagecore, statement wall pieces.

### 3. Pacific Northwest natural studio

Since the shop is based in Vancouver, British Columbia, a subtle Pacific Northwest identity could fit nicely.

Think misty forest, ocean textures, moss, driftwood, stone, calm natural photography. This could make the brand feel more local, authentic, and high-end.

Good for: custom commissions, local credibility, artisan positioning.

## Recommendation

Use **Light woodland gallery** as the main style, with small **dark forest accents**.

That gives the artist the widest appeal while still matching the moss, mushroom, bark, and cottagecore identity. The site should feel peaceful, handmade, natural, and slightly enchanted — not overly polished or corporate.
